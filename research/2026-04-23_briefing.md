# GEO Daily Briefing — 2026-04-23

**Theme of the day:** AI search is fragmenting into paid recommendation engines and zero-click answer machines, and brands still playing by 2024 SEO rules are paying for traffic that no longer exists.

---

## 1. ChatGPT ads expand to logged-out users

**Summary:** OpenAI has expanded its advertising pilot to include logged-out ChatGPT users, significantly broadening the addressable audience for AI-native ads. This move positions ChatGPT as a genuine discovery and search alternative where paid visibility will soon outrank organic brand mentions for users seeking recommendations.

**Source:** [Search Engine Land](https://searchengineland.com/chatgpt-ads-expand-to-logged-out-users-475377)

### Outreach Angles

**Professional Services:** When high-net-worth individuals ask ChatGPT for wealth management or legal advice, logged-out ad inventory means a competitor can now buy the singular recommendation slot—effectively making your decade of organic authority invisible to prospects who never see a traditional search result.
*Email hook:* If a prospective client asks ChatGPT for the best M&A attorney in Chicago, your competitor's ad could be the only name that answer mentions—logged-out users included.

**DTC/eCommerce:** ChatGPT's logged-out ad expansion turns AI conversations into pay-to-play product discovery channels; if a shopper asks for the best direct-to-consumer brand in your category and your competitor bought that impression, you just lost a customer before they even opened Google.
*Email hook:* Your customers are asking ChatGPT which mattress to buy before they ever touch Google, and now your competitor can pay to be the only brand that recommendation mentions.

## 2. Google addresses AI slop and query behavior shifts

**Summary:** In a recent discussion, Google Search lead Liz Reid outlined how AI Overviews are shifting user queries toward more complex, conversational patterns. She also highlighted growing concerns around 'AI slop'—mass-produced, low-quality AI content that risks degrading search trust and will likely face heavier algorithmic filtering.

**Source:** [Search Engine Land](https://searchengineland.com/google-liz-reid-ai-search-query-shifts-ai-slop-475324)

### Outreach Angles

**Professional Services:** The AI Overviews driving your traffic drops aren't just summarizing pages—they're changing the questions prospects ask, and if your firm is still publishing keyword-stuffed practice group pages instead of nuanced advisory content, you're answering obsolete queries while competitors capture the conversational searches.
*Email hook:* The AI-generated brief your junior associate published last week to 'boost SEO' is exactly the type of slop Google's Liz Reid said is clogging search—and it's why your firm just vanished from AI Overview citations.

**DTC/eCommerce:** Google's AI is now handling complex, multi-intent queries in single sessions, meaning product pages optimized for isolated keywords are getting bypassed entirely; brands without structured, comparison-friendly content are hemorrhaging high-intent buyers to AI-cited competitors.
*Email hook:* Your team is still chasing the keyword 'vegan protein,' but your buyers are now asking Google full questions like 'what's the cleanest plant protein that doesn't cause bloating'—and your product pages are invisible in those AI answers.
